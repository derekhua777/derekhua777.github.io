Some of my Recent Projects

Person Tracking Digital Twin

My lab partner and I created sensor fusion nodes on Nvidia Jetson devices and Intel NUCs. These nodes combined LiDAR, camera, and microphone sensors using a ROS pipeline in C++ and Python to perform backround subtraction of individual people and track their movements in an virtual representation of the lab space.

Micromouse- Maze Solving Robot

As part of the American Micromouse Competition, my partner and I created an autonomous maze solving robot. The robot uses IR sensors to detect the surrounding walls and optical encoders for the control system. We custom designed the PCB chasis and designed the Floodfill maze solving algorithm, receiving the judges choice award for our design.

Bruinfessions - Anonymous Confessions App

With a team of 5 UCLA students, we built a full stack application allowing users to post confessions. I built the backend using Google's Firebase realtime database along with the Google Auth Login. I also worked on displaying the posts along with the like and favoriting features allowing users to interact with posts.

Line Following Car

This was the final project for my intro to engineering class. I built a line following car using PID control loop. In the final competition my car placed in the top 10 out of the class of 100 students.

Cooking Ingredient Classifier

I designed a Convoultional Neural Network in Pytorch to perform visual recognition on cooking ingredients reaching a test accuracy of 96%. Based on the given ingredients, a few recommended recipes containing those ingredients are displayed to the user. This is still a work in progress so check back for updates!

FRC Robotics Captain

As captain of my school's FIRST Robotics Competition team I developed a cirriculum for 50+ students to prepare for competition season. During competition I was in charge of the robot design and led the prototyping and designing of multiple key components. We placed as finalists at the LA Regional in 2020.
